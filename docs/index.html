Pteridophytes have the PPG (Pteridophyte Phylogeny Group)

  • Had a late start
    • APG: 1998, 4 major updates
    • PPG: 2016, no updates yet
  • Community-based approach
    • APG: 13 authors
    • PPG: 94 authors

PPG is based on phylogeny

  • Primary criterion for recognizing taxa is monophyly

PPG is based on phylogeny

  • Primary criterion for recognizing taxa is monophyly

  • Based on many studies conducted at lower taxonomic levels

Towards PPG II

  • Since 2016, ca. 80 new names (genus and above) have been published

  • We need to discuss and decide whether to accept these names or not

  • Work towards PPG II, including names at genus and above started in May 2023 (Phase I)

A new discussion platform for PPG II

  • PPG I used email as as primary discussion tool
    • Not open
    • No permanent record
  • PPG II uses GitHub
    • All discussions are open and available in perpetuity

List of proposals (“issues”) is available at https://github.com/pteridogroup/ppg

Anyone can submit a proposal

  • Conditions
    • Must be a change relative to PPG I
    • Must be validly published according to the International Code of Nomenclature

All discussions are open

  • Anybody can view and comment
  • There is a 1-month discussion period before voting

Voting by Google forms

  • A \(2/3\) majority is required to pass

PPG II Phase 2

Phase 2: Curation of species-level data

  • PPG II includes data to species (PPG I only treated to genus)

  • There are too many species to vote on every change

  • Instead, PPG members volunteer as Curators who are responsible for species within their taxon of expertise (usually genus or family)

PPG manages data in parternship with WFO

  • World Flora Online（WFO） is a global database for plant names

  • PPG manages data for pteridophytes in WFO

  • WFO provides a taxonomic database editor called “Rhakhis”

Collaborative taxonomic editing in Rhakhis

  • Rhakhis enforces taxonomic rules

  • All editing done online, in the cloud

Benefits of the open, community-based approach

  • Lessens the workload
  • Ensures high-quality data
  • More frequent updates
    • We don’t have to wait another 10 years!!
    • PPG 2.0.1, 2.0.2, etc.

The majority of genera have at least one curator

Since starting Feb. 17 2026, we already have 59 curators covering 70.4% genera
